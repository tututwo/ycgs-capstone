# Limitations

This project is descriptive, not causal. It documents where wildfire closure burden appears in the available school closure records and how those patterns line up with county chronic absenteeism rates and school-year absence-reason measures.

The school closure dataset may not capture every closure or every partial disruption. Results should be interpreted as patterns in the available records, not a complete census of all wildfire-related educational disruption.

County aggregation can hide school-level variation. A county can have high total missed student-days because many students were exposed, while the most intense disruption may be concentrated in a smaller set of schools or districts.

Wildfire burden and absenteeism can both reflect other school and community conditions, including baseline attendance patterns, socioeconomic context, grade configuration, displacement, smoke exposure, district operations, and reporting practices.

CDE attendance files contain suppressed cells for privacy. Suppressed values are treated as missing, not zero.

The attendance analysis is limited to 2017-18 and 2018-19 because those years align with the school closure years selected for the main comparison. The 2019-20 absenteeism year is excluded because CDE marks that year unavailable/not valid due to COVID-era disruption.
