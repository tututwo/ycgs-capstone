# Final Summary

California wildfire closures in 2017-18 and 2018-19 were geographically concentrated. Missed student-days cluster in a relatively small group of counties, while chronic absenteeism and absence-reason patterns vary across affected places. These descriptive results point to counties and schools where attendance supports may be most useful after wildfire-related disruption.

The project uses wildfire missed student-days as the main burden measure because it combines how long schools closed with how many students were exposed. County and school summaries show that wildfire disruption is not evenly distributed across California.

The analysis should not be read as proof that wildfire closures caused chronic absenteeism. It is a pattern-finding project that identifies where disruption and attendance concerns overlap and where more detailed follow-up analysis could be valuable.
